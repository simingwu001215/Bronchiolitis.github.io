The Variation in Treatment for Bronchiolitis Among Clinicians and Hospitals

In the report, the analysis will focus on different treatment orders by examining the distribution of the number of patients seen and their corresponding percentages for each individual clinician. The dataset records child patients who are less than 12 months of age, presenting to the ED with respiratory illness due to Bronchiolitis. The objective is to investigate how variation is displayed at both the clinician and hospital levels. Users can interact with the scatter plots by zooming in and out. Additionally, if there is a specific clinician ID available, the "Search for Clinician" feature can be used to target that clinician's data. If the visualization layout appears too spread out, adjusting the zoom settings on the PC can provide a better view.

In the antibiotics visualisation, the barchart reveals several insights:

  • Antibiotics are widely used as a treatment for Bronchiolitis.
  • The proportion of patients who received antibiotics in each hospital ranges from around 6.75% to 10%.
  • Most of the orders were not given by the treating clinician.

Adrenaline is cautiously used as a treatment for Bronchiolitis, with around 1% of patients in each hospital receiving it. The most significant insight from the bar chart is:

  • Most of the treatments were ordered by the treating ED clinician.

The blood test is widely used in each hospital, with a very high proportion of patients receiving it as a treatment for Bronchiolitis. The bar chart reveals that blood tests are widely used as a treatment for Bronchiolitis in each hospital, with these features:

  • proportion rate ranging from 26% to 27%.
  • Casey and Dandenong demonstrate a larger proportion of orders made by the treating clinician compared to MMC-Clayton.

The barchat reveals corticosteroids were widely accessed as treatment of Bronchiolitis in each hospital with proportion rate arond 8% to 10%.

  • Most of the orders of corticosteriods are ordered by treating ED clinician.

The barchat reveals the variation of the proportion of patients received salbutamol in different hospital is relatively large.

  • In MMC-Clayton, only 1.77% of patients had received salbutamol for Bronchiolitis.
  • In the meanwhile, the proportion of patients had received salbutamol are 5.35% and 7.44% in Casey and Dandenong respectively.
  • In general, most of the treatments are given by treating ED clinician.
