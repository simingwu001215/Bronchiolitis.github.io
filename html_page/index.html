The Variation Between Clinicians and Hospitals

The main targets of the report are to investigate how different orders are applied as a treatment of Bronchiolitis and how the treatments are delivered at the hospital level with a specific group of patients who are under 12 months. The conclusion can be illustrated by the following distributions of individual clinician and also the overall proportions compared to the size of the hospital.

In the Antibiotics visualisation, the violin plot reveals several insights:

  • Dandenong: Most of antibiotics treatment for Bronchiolitis were done by someone else.
  • MMC-Clayton: Low usage were directly applied by doctors, but the proportion of antibiotics that directly were applied to doctor is significantly greater than the other two.
  • Casey: Low usage were directly applied by doctors.

Summary: Antibiotics is a widely-use for Bronchiolitis, most of the applications are applied by different clinicians, MMC- Clayton demonstrates higher proportion application that were done by doctors.

Overall, adrenaline is cautiously applied as a treatment for Bronchiolitis under 2% applied rate in each hospital and requires stricter guideline such that most of the applications are done by doctors.

  • In Dandenong Hospital, only a small proportion of access of adrenaline were done by different clinicians.

Blood Test is a conventional treatment to Bronchiolitis with over 25% of patients with Bronchiolitis received.

  • The proportion of patients received blood test as treatment in MMC - Clayton is lower compared the other two hospitals.

Corticosteroids is a conventional treatment to Bronchiolitis with over 8% of patients with Bronchiolitis received.

  • Most of the treatment were done by doctors compared to other clinicians.
